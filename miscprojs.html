Miscellaneous Projects

To everyone that's here, hello! This page is just a little casual something where I just show an anthology of some random projects and things that I think are neat or funny, so I hope you enjoy!

RAWKET!!

First, I want to show a little rocket I made back in high school. I followed a YouTube video where a guy made a sugar rocket, where the motor is made with a mixture of sugar, the fuel, and potassium nitrate, the oxidizer. This was the summer after my senior year in high school, so I really only had one shot to get my first hands-on foray into aerospace off the ground (no pun intended). You can see the video below!


(It didn't work)

Through the Fire and Flames

Next, I wanted to show all of you a more successful project: a home made flamethrower. >:)

I used an old fire extinguisher I found in my garage, drilled in a hole and shoved in a tire valve, filled it with lighter fluid, and used a bicycle pump to pressurize it. To light it, I just hose clamped a bent copper pipe with a wick from one of those tiki torches soaked with lighter fluid stuffed inside onto the fire extinguisher. Bada bing, bada boom, FIRE!


Call Me a Pilot (I'm very much not a pilot)

Lastly, I have a few pictures from when I flew a plane! I was part of the Civil Air Patrol, and one of the things you get to do when you first join is fly a plane (this was the main reason I joined so I was practically jumping out of my seat when it happened). We flew a Cessna 182 Skyhawk, and the pilot was the one who did take-off and landing. However, once we got to cruising altitude, he handed over the controls for a few minutes, and this just solidified my desire to eventually get a pilot's license. To this day, if I ever have to tell anyone a "fun fact" about myself for an icebreaker, this is still the thing I always go back to!
